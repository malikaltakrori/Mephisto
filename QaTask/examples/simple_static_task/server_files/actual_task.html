This task is part of a study on text anonymization techniques. An anonymization technique modifies a sentence to hide the writing style of the author. The technique, however, must preserve the content of the original text. Content preservation means that we need to maintain the original meaning of the sentence and convey the same information as intended by the original author.

Your role in this task is to help us evaluate the modified sentences in terms of content preservation. We are not interested in how fluent the sentence is. If some information is in the original sentence then it should also be in the modified sentence. If there is extra information in the modified sentence that does not contradict the original sentence, then that does not violate content preservation.

The passage is provided to clarify the context. You do not have to read it but you are free to do so. The examples, on the other hand, are suggested as guidelines to help you rate the modified sentences.

The rating system:

When rating the modified sentences for content preservation, we ask you to choose one of the three options below.

  1. All the information in the origial sentnece is in the modified sentence. (Content fully preserved)

  2. Some information from the original sentence is in the modified sentence but some is missing. (Content partially preserved)

  3. Important information in the origial sentence is missing from the modified sentence. (Content not preserved)

Examples:

Below, we provide some examples on each one of the rating options.

1. All the information in the origial sentnece is in the modified sentence. (Content fully preserved)
a. Adding typos, switching the letter case in random places, or adding/changing/removing punctuation.
Original Modified version(s)
I was walking to the market. I was walking ot the market.
I was walking To the Market.
I was walking tothe market.
I was walking to the ‘market’.
Explanation: As a reader, you can notice that these are typos, ignore/correct them and still understand the original meaning.
b. Replacing words with other words that fit the context.
Original Modified version(s)
I was walking to the mall. I was walking to the shop.
I was walking towards the Market.
I was walking towards the Supermarket. (Note the multiple changes)
Explanation:All the sentences mean that I walked to a place for shopping. While there are differences between mall, shop, market, and supermarket we consider these differences as minor ones.
c. Paraphrasing the sentence.
Original Modified version(s)
I was walking to the market. I took a walk to the market.
I went to the market on foot.
Explanation: After paraphrasing, we can understand that I went to market by walking.
I ate the cake. The cake was eaten by me.
Explanation: After paraphrasing, we understand that I did eat the cake.
d. Adding information that does not contradict or modify the original meaning.
Original Modified version(s)
I was walking to the market. I enjoyed walking towards the market.
Explanation: While the extra information (I enjoyed) does not appear in the original sentence, having it does not contradict the original meaning which is “I walked to the market”.
2. Some information from the original sentence is in the modified sentence but some is missing. (Content partially preserved)
a. The context helped figuring out the meaning.
Original Modified version(s)
She was walking around in the market. She was walking around from the market.
Context: Anny went shopping. <the original sentence was here>. During that walk she stopped at different places.
Explanation: By looking at the context, specifically, that she was stopping at shops, we understand that she is still in the market walking around in it.
b. The sentence has multiple modifications, but only some of them preserve the meaning.
Original Modified version(s)
While the "Brain Drain" traditionally occurs due to a lesser-developed country not being able to offer competitive compensation to in-country doctors, conflict can have the same effect. While the "brain drive" is traditionally because a less developed country is unable to offer competitive compensation to local doctors, the conflict can have the same effect.
Context: A second major issue, and one that has long-term post-conflict implications, is of doctor migration out of the country, also known as the “Brain Drain”. <the original sentence was here>
Explanation: There are several modifications in this sentence.
The following ones do not change the meaning:
Traditionally occurs due to --> is traditionally because      Not being able --> is unable      conflict --> the conflict
The following changes on the other hand, have a slightly different meaning:
In-country --> local : In country means a national level, while local could mean the neighborhood, the community, or the city levels.
Lesser-developed --> less developed : the change, less developed is more negative than lesser-developed.
Finally, brain drain --> brain drive : The term "brain drain" is defined in the context
In both these last cases, there is a loss of information, but some of the original informaiton is still preserved.
c. The sentence contains multiple changes some of them do not fit the context
Original Modified version(s)
I was walking to the market then stopped to tie my shoes. I was going to the market tehn stopped to link my shoes.
Explanation: The first typo (tehn) is minor. The second change: “link my shoes” is very clear. We stop to tie our shoes and not to link them. One can infer the original meaning by recalling that we tie our shoes but using ‘link’ is clearly wrong.
3. Important information in the origial sentence is missing from the modified sentence. (Content not preserved)
a. Adding/removing negation
Original Modified version(s)
I was in a hurry. I was not in a hurry.
Context: Today I left work late. <the original sentence was here>. I got home at 8pm.
Explanation: Adding ‘not’ flips the meaning of the original sentence, ‘not’ appears natural in the sentence, and the context does not show anything wrong with the modified sentence.
b. Switching prepositions
Original Modified version(s)
I was walking to the market. I was walking from the market.
Explanation: replacing ‘to’ with ‘from’ flips the meaning of the original sentence
c. The sentence contains multiple change, and the context does not help retrieving the original meaning.
Original Modified version(s)
I took a bus and went to the market to find that it was open. I took a nap and went to the market to find that it was closed.
Context: Today I left work late and had to buy stuff. <the original sentence was here>. Later, I came back home.
Explanation: The two changes, “took a nap” and “was closed” make the meaning very different. The context does not help infer the original meaning.
d. The paraphrasing causes a loss of information.
Original Modified version(s)
This problem only surfaced in Iraq after the war. This problem appeared only after the war in Iraq.
Context: The black market for weapons has become very accessible. <the original sentence was here>.
Explanation: In the original sentence, the problem is in Iraq. In the modified sentence the problem could be in a different place (such as a neighboring country) but is caused by the war in Iraq.
